# Themes Identified in RQ1

**AI integration in cloud solution architectures**

Total participants: N = 57

| Theme | n | % |
|-------|---|---|
| Technical challenges, quality, security, or cost | 40 | 70.2 |
| Positive perception / pedagogical value | 32 | 56.1 |
| Data, analysis, and real-time decision-making | 29 | 50.9 |
| Automation, productivity, or optimization | 27 | 47.4 |
| Modularity, scalability, or decoupling | 26 | 45.6 |
| Explicit mention of architectural styles | 23 | 40.4 |

## Interpretation

The most recurrent theme was the identification of **technical challenges, quality, security, or cost**, present in 40 responses (70.2%). This result indicates that students did not perceive AI only as an opportunity for innovation, but also as a source of architectural constraints.

Students' perception of AI was structured by a tension between value and viability. On one hand, they recognized the pedagogical and professional potential of AI; on the other hand, they highlighted that its adoption introduces costs, risks, latency, dependency on external services, and integration complexity.

Students associated AI with:
- **Data and real-time decision-making** (50.9%)
- **Automation** (47.4%)
- **Modularity and scalability** (45.6%)
- **Specific architectural styles** (40.4%)

This combination suggests an initial understanding of AI as a component that needs to be positioned in the architecture, encapsulated in services, integrated into data flows, and supported by styles such as microservices, serverless, EDA, and BFF.
