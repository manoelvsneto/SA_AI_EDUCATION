# Themes Identified in RQ2

**Architectural modeling, understanding and communication of decisions**

Total participants: N = 57

| Theme | n | % |
|-------|---|---|
| Understanding, visualization, and clarity | 48 | 84.2 |
| Documentation, organization, and standardization | 46 | 80.7 |
| Levels of abstraction / C4 Model | 39 | 68.4 |
| Flows, interactions, and UML | 36 | 63.2 |
| Architectural decisions and justifications | 35 | 61.4 |
| Communication and alignment between stakeholders | 28 | 49.1 |

## Interpretation

Architectural modeling was the most consistent axis of the experience:
- **Understanding, visualization, and clarity** appeared in 48 responses (84.2%)
- **Documentation, organization, and standardization** in 46 responses (80.7%)

Students attributed to models a broader role than the simple production of documentary artifacts:
- **C4 Model** was associated with understanding through levels of abstraction (68.4%)
- **UML** with detailing flows, interactions, and behaviors (63.2%)
- **arc42** with organizing and standardizing decisions

Together, these elements supported understanding, clarity, justification, and communication.

Modeling functioned as **cognitive and communicational mediation**, helping students to:
- Decompose the complexity of solutions with AI and cloud
- Make choices explicit
- Make architecture more discussable among different actors

This finding reinforces that, in SA courses, models such as C4, UML, and arc42 should not be treated only as final deliveries, but as instruments of architectural reasoning throughout the learning process.
