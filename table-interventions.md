# Didactic Interventions Derived from Findings

| Finding | Didactic implication | Recommended intervention |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Students recognize AI as relevant, but rarely mention MLOps and drift. | AI needs to be taught as a lifecycle, not just as a component or API. | Practical activity on deployment, versioning, monitoring, *model drift*, retraining, and model rollback. |
| Security, cost, and latency appear as challenges. | *Trade-offs* must be made explicit and evaluated. | Use of ADRs, quality scenarios, and comparative analysis between architectural alternatives. |
| C4, UML, and arc42 support clarity and communication. | Modeling should be integrated into the decision process, not just the final delivery. | Incremental documentation deliveries with peer reviews and teacher feedback. |
| Event-driven and BFF appear less than microservices and serverless. | Integration patterns need reinforcement in AI scenarios. | Exercise with domain events, queues, consumers, AI service, and BFF for different channels. |
| Practical learning is valued. | Realistic projects increase meaning and transfer to professional contexts. | Projects with mandatory constraints on cost, security, observability, privacy, and scalability. |
